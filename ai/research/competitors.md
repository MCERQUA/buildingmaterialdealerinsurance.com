# Competitor Research — Building Material Dealer Insurance

**Method:** DataForSEO SERP Live for "lumber yard insurance", "hardware store insurance", "building material dealer insurance" + manual review of known specialty program carriers. Most results are either (a) national program-marketing agencies (Berkley, AmWins, USI), (b) generic "contractor insurance" lead-gen aggregators, or (c) big-box retail insurance pages. Very few sites target the dealer/lumber-yard buyer by name.

## SERP Landscape (what currently ranks)
| Type | Examples | Weakness |
|------|----------|----------|
| Specialty program carriers | Berkley Program "Lumber & Building Material", AmWins, USI | Carrier-direct, not broker; no quote form, no educational content, no nationwide broker reach |
| Aggregators / lead-gen | Insureon, Next, Hiscox, CoverWallet | Generic small-business insurance; no dealer-specific copy; treats lumber yard same as coffee shop |
| Big-box retail | The Home Depot / Lowe's vendor pages | Not insurance-buying intent; irrelevant |
| Regional agents | Single-state independent agents | No nationwide licensing; thin content |

## Top Reference Competitors (structure + CTA patterns)
1. **Insureon.com** — strong trust signals (5-star reviews, "X businesses insured"), multi-step quote, deep-by-trade landing pages. *Their weakness: no lumber-yard/dealer depth.* We match their trust + quote UX, beat them on dealer specificity.
2. **Berkley Program — Lumber & Building Material Dealers** — the gold-standard program carrier. Names the buyer exactly. *Weakness: no broker quote path, no blog, no FAQ.* We mirror their buyer-targeting and add the broker/quote/content layer.
3. **Hiscox small business** — polished quote flow, clean trust bar. *Weakness: generic.* We borrow the trust-bar + quote-flow polish, not the generic copy.
4. **Next Insurance** — by-trade landing pages with a single CTA ("Get a quote in minutes"). *Weakness: surface-level.* We match the by-trade landing page model and go deeper per coverage line.

## What winners do (we adopt)
- Name the buyer explicitly in H1 (not "contractor insurance" — "lumber yard insurance").
- Lead with the specific exposures (fire, inventory, forklift, delivery fleet, product liability).
- Multi-step or multi-field quote form with trade-specific dropdowns.
- Trust bar: years in business, states licensed, claims-response SLA, carrier ratings.
- FAQ + educational blog (PAA capture).

## What losers miss (we own)
- Product liability for dealers (selling others' goods).
- Inventory / stock coverage + actual-cash-value vs replacement-cost nuance.
- Inland marine for the delivery run.
- Equipment breakdown (kilns, compressors, dust collection, forklift chargers).
- Forklift + loading-dock workers comp class codes.
